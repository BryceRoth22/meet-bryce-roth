Meet Bryce Roth Milestone 1

Purpose, Audience and Goal

Purpose: This project will be a professional website showcasing my web development skills.

Audience: The design of the site is directed to the professional IT community within any
size of business. The target audiences are recruiters, HR managers and other IT professionals seeking
web developers.

Goal: The goal is to showcase different projects, collaborations on projects, previous
relevant work history and other soft skills. All the showcased items should be geared towards the IT
professional who is looking to add someone to their team of developers or help with a specific project. The
end goal is to gain employment using the skills learned in Deep Dive.

Persona

Name: Harvey Rutherford

Age: 43

Profession or Role: Hiring manager looking for mid-level web developers.

Technology: Works remotely at times and has a company provided laptop with a docking station,
wireless keyboard/mouse and a second monitor. Company provided equipment is at least a couple years old. He
has an iPhone 14 Pro with the largest screen available.

Attitudes and Behaviors: Because of the busy days working to find potential candidates for
various job positions in the company and keeping up with family and friends, Harvey conducts as much business and
scheduling of work, family and friends on his iPhone. He wants most (possibly all) his work and social interactions
to be done on his iPhone if it can't be done in person. Harvey has several positions to fill and wants to be able
to quickly determine if a candidate will have the skills and attitude needed to do the job.

Frustrations and Needs: Frustrated that he is hiring web developers that will have newer, more
up-to-date equipment than him. Harvey does not like to use the companies equipment since it is old and not
reliable. Needs access to potential web developers and be able to quickly see that they have the necessary and
basic skills to be a mid-level web developer.

Goals: Finding and filling the mid-level web developer open positions quickly. He will need to
determine if a candidate is worth moving forward with an interview or not in the least amount of time possible. He
does not have time to dig around with each candidate to find the information he needs to make a decision.

User Story: As a tech savvy hiring manager, I need to find qualified candidates for my open web
developer positions on my iPhone.
